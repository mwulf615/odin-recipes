Ingredients

  • Butter.I use salted butter, but unsalted butter would also be great. I just recommend tasting the dough to ensure that it's salted to your liking.
  • White Sugar. You can use granulated white sugar or organic cane sugar.
  • Brown Sugar. I use light brown sugar in this recipe for the best results.
  • Flour. I recommend using an unbleached, all-purpose flour to make these cookies. Many readers have used gluten-free all-purpose flour with excellent results.
  • Sea Salt. Sea salt is different that table salt, so if you use salt other than sea salt I recommend testing the recipe first with 1/2 tsp and then adjusting to your taste.
  • Chocolate ChipsSometimes I use chocolate chunks, chopped chocolate, or chocolate chips. It doesn't matter what you use, and long as you use 2 cups.

Steps

Making the Cookie Dough

  1. Combine the dry ingredients in a medium size bowl.
  2. Creame together the butter and sugars. It helps to soften the butter beforehand.
  3. Once the butter/sugar mixture is beaten well, add the eggs, and vanilla and beat to combine.
  4. Add in the dry ingredients and stir until combined.
  5. Add in the chocolate chips until even distrubuted throughtout the dough.

Portion & Bake

  1. I highly recommend using a cookie scoop to portion the dough instead of eyeballing.
  2. Scoop each portion and place roll the portion into a ball.
  3. Place each ball evenly apart on a baking sheet. (About 1.5 to 2 inches aprts)
  4. Bake at 375 degress for 8 - 10 minutes.
  5. Allow the cookes to rest for a few minutes.
